## Design Context

### Users
This extension is for Chess.com players who want a hard stop after a loss to avoid tilt, revenge queues, and low-quality rematches. They use it in the middle of active play sessions, so the UI needs to communicate lock state instantly with almost no reading.

### Brand Personality
The product should feel disciplined, polished, and quietly encouraging. It should borrow the product confidence and familiar game-room tone of Chess.com without pretending to be an official Chess.com feature.

### Aesthetic Direction
Use a Chess.com-native visual language: dark app-shell surfaces, moss-green action tones, muted stone neutrals, compact information density, and board-adjacent textures used sparingly. Favor an interface that feels like a real Chess.com panel or modal, not a decorative fantasy board or a generic extension dashboard.

### Design Principles
1. Status must be legible in one glance, especially lock state and unlock timing.
2. The UI should feel like it belongs beside Chess.com's existing play, review, and coach surfaces.
3. Decorative chess motifs should support hierarchy, not dominate it.
4. Copy should sound calm and coaching, never dramatic or punitive.
5. The extension must stay recognizably `No Tilt Chess`, even when the visual language is strongly Chess.com-inspired.
